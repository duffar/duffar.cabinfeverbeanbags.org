Fighter Round

Last year, for the first time at our event, we honored four local kids that are battling cancer (and kicking it's butt) by making them Honorary Captains of the tournament. By doing this, we brought the focus back to why we're joining forces and raising lots of money-to get rid of this terrible disease called cancer. The kids played a game of bean bags in our Fighter/Survivor Round along with a local celebrity of their choice. We also shared some stories about these strong kids' battles with this awful disease.

Last Years Honorary Captains were:

Ana vanKoeverden is a 7 year old (whose playing partner is Kris Davidson, principal at Longfellow Elementary School) who has pilocytic astrocytoma in the brainstem (a.k.a. brain tumor). Ana likes to run, wild and free. She's an on-the-go kinda kid who loves being active. She is known for singing "hey, soul sister" in a BIG, off-tune voice. She dreams of spending every minute of summer camping and swimming. She delights in her friends, and is happiest when she's with others. She likes American Girl dolls, the color pink, and hoarding stuff in her bedroom. She likes animals, both big and small, especially if they are snuggly. Ana has a care-giving heart and loves helping take care of babies and toddlers.




Austin Hosfeld is a 5 year old from Mantorville fighting leukemia since being diagnosed in August 2011. Austin is playing with his Secret Service Agent uncle Erich Schulenberg and Fire Fighter Kris Jungels. Austin is an active, sweet, spunky, energetic little guy who knows what he likes :) , enjoys being outside...especially on the boat, helping daddy, and playing with mommy. One thing is for sure...he LOVES cars! Whether it's the movie Cars, toy cars , race cars...he just loves cars. He especially loves fast ones like the stock cars the four local race car drivers will bring to the event! The drivers joining us include
  • #40 Nick Kramer
  • #19 Troy Voth
  • #77t Casey Trom
  • #35 Jerry Bloom






Hunter Mundfrom (whose partner is Marcus Sherels!) is a 9 year old from Spring Valley who has been battling leukemia for over one year. He loves baseball, especially the Minnesota Twins. Hunter's dad brought him to his first Twins game on his 3rd birthday. He fell in love with baseball that day. Hunter became a fan of Torii HUNTER of the Detroit Tigers (who played for the Twins at the time) when he saw his own name on the back of Torii's jersey that day. Hunter has gone to a Twins game on or near his birthday ever since. Hunter also enjoys golf. In fact, he is planning to host a golf benefit this summer to raise money to support childhood cancer research! We encourage all of you to join us when the date and location are finalized!.






Wyatt Deno is a 9 year old from Kasson who has battled a malignant brain tumor since November 2011. He is currently in remission and will continue to get scans and tests done every three months for the next two years, and even though the time between tests will lengthen, there is continued risk and he will be regularly tested for the next 5 years! Wyatt is an avid wrestler and inspired his teammates and the entire community to form Wyatt's Warriors to help him beat this disease. He also enjoys watching funny movies...especially ones featuring Jack Black or Adam Sandler! Wyatt sports a huge smile and is a funny man himself and has continued to be full of jokes...never missing an opportunity to make his doctors, nurses, family and friends laugh during his treatments. Wyatt is a sweet, caring young man and has a giggle that is contagious, but don't be fooled, his scar is proof that he is one tough little guy who shall not be messed with on the wrestling mat...Go Wyatt! Wyatt's partner is his Papa, Ted Perzynski, who has become his best friend after spending many days and nights with Wyatt while in the hospital for his cancer treatments...Papas are awesome!!
